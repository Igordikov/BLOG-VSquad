Why Outsource Game Art?

Outsourcing is popular for any game studio now, including art companies making AAA games Because game art outsourcing has several advantages. You can get all the specialists you need: art, animation, 3D, 2D, you name it. And what’s good about it is that studios don’t have to look for each separate expert online, the outsource company has them all. 

The other advantage of using an outsource is that it saves time and resources. You can hand over the production process, like concept art or 3D modeling and animation, to an external team. So in-house devs can work on game mechanics and code. If you have deadlines or other issues it’s a perfect option.  

Flexibility is another reason why outsourcing is favored by many developers. Studios can collaborate with partners that match the desired art style of their project, whether that involves a hyperrealistic look or a more cartoon-like approach. This ability to choose the right aesthetic partner is key to creating a unique and immersive player experience.

A crucial part of the outsourcing process is the initial estimation phase. One of the objectives for this phase is to understand the project's needs, including the art style and asset requirements, which helps set clear expectations. The studio then defines the scope of the work, specifying deliverables and any allowed revisions. Based on the project requirements, they allocate the necessary resources, ensuring that the right talent is available throughout the production. Estimating the timeline involves breaking down the project into phases, assigning appropriate durations to each. Cost calculation follows, offering a transparent budget plan that minimizes unexpected financial burdens.

Outsourcing game art can help studios to make a good game more quickly. They can adapt to different visual styles, and maintain quality. But you need to choose the right outsourcing partner that can do that.

How to Choose the Best Game Art Outsourcing Studio

Consider several things if you want to choose the right game art outsourcing studio. First, look at their portfolio. Because in such a way, the company shows its skills. Are they able to make what you require? A strong portfolio with lots of works like 3D character design services, animation, and others is what you’re looking for. Sometimes an outsource studio doesn’t have to work with some big names to prove their worth. Just look at the quality of their art and decide for yourself. 

Another thing to check is client reviews and testimonials. You’ll need to look for the company's reliability and how they treat deadlines. This is why feedback from previous clients is so important. Just look it up on any search engine you have, don’t look for feedback on the official outsource company’s website. Studios with positive reviews often demonstrate trustworthiness and a history of successful collaborations.

The video game outsourcing companies should definitely have experience working with modern and basic tech, like 3D modeling. They should understand technical requirements of a project so later on it can be integrated with engines like Unity or Unreal Engine. You want a smoother production and to maintain the intended quality throughout the game development process.

Also, a studio should adapt to the latest trends in 3D character design. They should do their research and see what kind of characters for what kind of game is suitable the most. At the same time, they should make something completely unique. Artists can get inspiration from some other games, but the result should not be a copyright lawsuit. A studio with these capabilities can be a valuable partner.
